About Me

Ciao! I'm Federico, an undergraduate researcher from the vibrant city of Milan, Italy, currently furthering my academic journey at Northeastern University. My fascination with computer science began in the playful realms of childhood, where at the tender age of 8, I began experimenting with simple coding challenges, sparking a lifelong commitment to bits and bytes. Over the years, my enthusiasm has found its niche at the intersections of artificial intelligence, programming languages, and security.

I am proud to contribute to the research community under the guidance of Prof. Arjun Guha at the Northeastern University Programming Research Lab (NUPRL). Being an undergraduate hasn't limited my endeavors; I've had the privilege of publishing in several esteemed research venues and collaborating with leading experts in the field. These achievements stand as a testament to both my unwavering commitment and the supportive environment at NUPRL.

Outside the university, I've been fortunate to further hone my skills and insights as a research intern at pioneering companies such as Roblox and Trail of Bits. Through these experiences, I aim to blend academic rigor with real-world applications, consistently pushing the boundaries of what's possible in computer science.

From time to time, I enjoy writing my thoughts in free-form prose on my blog.
